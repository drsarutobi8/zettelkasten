## Pages worth a permanent note
- p.292 - 12.2 Niklas Luhmann’s Card Index is a result of his reading notes, his own wisdom, and his ideas of next projects.
- p.293 - steps of collection	
	- **Step 1 — Read with the slip box in mind**  - pattern-matching against existing notes. **Step 2 — Raw jottings**  -  Keywords + page numbers + possibly a note of _which existing card_ this connects to. 
	- **Step 3 — Sit down with both literatures** The new permanent note is written at the intersection
- p.297 - filing system
	- looks like hierarchy but actually no fixed hierarchy -> this is by his choice. Luhmann didn't want the structure to constrain his ideas
	- new notes growing based on existing notes. So the numbering is inwardly
	- the position of card could be anywhere and the important level is independent
	- one idea could be split into many permanent notes based on different context. Therefore they could be in different branch with the content related to front card
